Our Projects
Human-Robot Interaction

Our lab explores fundamental aspects of human-robot interaction with a specific focus on healthcare applications. We investigate how robots can effectively communicate with humans, understand social cues, and respond appropriately in healthcare settings.

Current projects include developing models for robot empathy, designing intuitive interfaces for healthcare professionals to program assistive robots, and studying long-term human-robot relationships in care environments.

Emotionally Intelligent Robotics

We are developing robots capable of recognizing and responding to human emotions, which is crucial for effective healthcare assistance. Our research combines computer vision, natural language processing, and machine learning to create robots that can detect emotional states and respond appropriately.

This work includes creating datasets of emotional expressions in healthcare contexts, developing multimodal emotion recognition algorithms, and designing robot behaviors that convey empathy and understanding.

Assistive Technologies

Our assistive technology research focuses on creating robots that can help individuals with physical limitations, cognitive impairments, or age-related challenges. We design robotic systems that can assist with daily activities, provide medication reminders, support rehabilitation exercises, and enhance independence.

Current projects include developing adaptive learning algorithms that personalize assistance based on individual needs and preferences, creating intuitive control systems for assistive robots, and designing robots that can safely navigate home environments.
